Overview

Call of Duty: Black Ops Cold War is a first-person shooter developed by Treyarch and Raven Software, published by Activision. It was released on November 13, 2020 for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S, and PC via Battle.net.

Set during the early 1980s Cold War period, the game serves as a direct sequel to the original Black Ops (2010) and features a mix of historical events and fictional espionage.


Campaign


The campaign follows a CIA operative codenamed "Bell," working alongside iconic character Russell Adler to track down a Soviet agent known only as "Perseus," who threatens to detonate nuclear devices across Western Europe. The narrative incorporates real historical figures and events, blending classified Cold War history with a tense, branching storyline.

Players can make key choices throughout the campaign that affect the ending, giving it a degree of replayability uncommon for the franchise.


Multiplayer


Cold War's multiplayer retained much of the fast-paced, boots-on-ground gameplay the Black Ops series is known for, while introducing new modes such as Fireteam: Dirty Bomb and the return of fan-favourite Combined Arms. The Create-A-Class system was reworked with a "Wildcard" system allowing more flexibility in loadout building.

The game also continued the cross-progression system established by Modern Warfare, linking weapons and operators into Warzone.


Zombies


Cold War introduced a major overhaul to the Zombies mode, launching with the "Die Maschine" map and introducing a new Dark Aether storyline separate from the previous Aether story arc. It added an Outbreak mode — a large open-world Zombies experience across Fireteam maps — which became one of the most popular additions to the mode in years.


Warzone Integration

Cold War was the first title to fully integrate into Call of Duty: Warzone, introducing its weapons, operators, and content directly into the battle royale. Season 3 of Cold War brought the massive Verdansk '84 event, retooling the original Warzone map with a 1980s aesthetic to align with the game's setting.

Engine

A short breakdown about the Black Ops Cold War engine.


While Warzone continued to run on Infinity Ward's IW8 engine, Black Ops Cold War itself was built on a heavily modified version of the Black Ops 4 engine, developed internally at Treyarch. This created a unique cross-engine integration challenge — Cold War content had to be ported and adapted to run inside IW8 for Warzone compatibility. Despite this, the integration was largely seamless from a player-facing perspective, with shared progression, weapons, and seasonal content.

Development

Black Ops Cold War had one of the most turbulent development histories in the franchise, shaped by studio conflict, a compressed timeline, and the global COVID-19 pandemic.


Origins & Studio Upheaval

The game's origins trace back to 2018, when Activision assigned Raven Software and Sledgehammer Games as co-leads on the 2020 Call of Duty title — breaking the established three-year rotation between Infinity Ward, Treyarch, and Sledgehammer that had defined the franchise since 2012. The two studios argued frequently during development, with sources describing the project as "a mess."

In May 2019, Activision assigned Treyarch to take creative leadership of the project, with Sledgehammer and Raven repositioned as support studios. This left Treyarch with roughly two years to ship — a full year less than their usual development cycle — and some at the studio were already bracing for brutal overtime hours, similar to what they faced during Black Ops 4.


Impact of COVID-19

The reduced development time was almost assuredly further complicated by the COVID-19 pandemic, which impacted studios across the industry — yet despite early speculation, Cold War was not significantly delayed. Workflow was significantly decreased due to COVID-19, making it much harder to work on a project of this scale, and as a result leftover and placeholder content was not cleaned up as thoroughly as in previous entries.

The pressure showed at launch. Cold War shipped with only 8 multiplayer maps and a campaign critics described as bare-bones, alongside a long list of technical issues including poor PC optimization, subpar audio, and bad hit detection. Many in the community felt the game had been pushed out before it was ready.


Budget & Scale

Despite the turbulent production, the game was a massive undertaking financially. According to a court filing by Activision's head of creative Patrick Kelly, Treyarch and Raven Software ultimately spent over $700 million in development costs over the game's lifecycle — among the highest ever publicly disclosed for any video game. By December 2024, Black Ops Cold War had sold 30 million copies.
