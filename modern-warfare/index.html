Overview

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019) is one of many CoD titles released year after year by Activision. It was released on October 25, 2019 only on consoles such as PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, as well as PC (Battle.Net only)

It later expanded to Steam in 2023 and became available on Xbox Game Pass.It later expanded to Steam in 2023 and became available on Xbox Game Pass.


Campaign


The narrative follows a gritty, realistic reimagining of the series, shifting away from linear, globe-trotting action toward tactical, morally grey warfare. Players assume the roles of CIA and British SAS operators (including iconic returning characters like Captain Price) teaming up with rebel forces in the fictional country of Urzikstan to recover stolen chemical weapons and combat a rogue Russian paramilitary group.


Multiplayer


MW2019 was widely praised for overhauling the franchise's core mechanics by introducing the IW 8.0 engine, delivering highly photorealistic environments, dynamic bullet penetration, and deep weapon customization. It removed traditional DLC Season Passes and introduced the "Seasons" Battle Pass structure


Warzone

Call of Duty: Warzone is a massive, free-to-play battle royale video game developed by Infinity Ward and Raven Software. Originally launching on March 10, 2020, it revolutionized the franchise by linking multiple premium titles into a single, unified ecosystem. It quickly exploded in popularity, crossing 100 million players in just over a year

Engine

A short breakdown about the MW2019 (IW8) Engine.


Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019) and Call of Duty: Warzone (2020) were both built upon the IW8 engine, heavily revamped from the previous IW7 engine built for Infinite Warfare. The engine heavily improved upon aspects such as visuals, as well as internally overhauling their protection systems such as Arxan, later being cracked and documented with the help of CWHook.
