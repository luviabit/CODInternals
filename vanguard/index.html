Overview

Call of Duty: Vanguard is a first-person shooter developed by Sledgehammer Games and published by Activision, released on November 5, 2021. It is the 18th installment in the Call of Duty franchise and Sledgehammer's third lead title in the series, following Advanced Warfare (2014) and WWII (2017).

Set during World War II, the game's narrative centres on the formation of the world's first special forces unit, fighting across four major theatres of war. It received mixed reviews — praised for campaign visuals and multiplayer depth, but criticised for its Zombies mode and perceived lack of innovation.


Campaign


Vanguard's campaign follows four soldiers from different nations — each with their own combat speciality — who are brought together to form a prototype special forces unit tasked with stopping a Nazi remnant known as Project Phoenix from ensuring the Third Reich's ideology survives the war's end.

Unlike previous WWII-era Call of Duty titles that focused almost exclusively on the Western Front, Vanguard covers lesser-seen theatres including Stalingrad, the Pacific, North Africa, and occupied France. Campaign director Dave Swanson described the intention as taking a more intimate, character-driven approach compared to the traditional globe-hopping formula.


Multiplayer


Multiplayer introduced several notable additions to the formula: a new environmental destruction system that accumulated visible structural damage throughout a match, the ability to charge through doors, and 20 launch maps — a notably larger offering than Cold War's 8. A Champion Hill tournament mode was also added as a new competitive format.

Gunsmith returned in an expanded form, carrying over the deep weapon customisation framework established in MW2019 and allowing cross-progression with Warzone's weapon pool.


Zombies


Zombies was co-developed by Treyarch, extending the Dark Aether storyline introduced in Black Ops Cold War. The launch map "Der Anfang" blended round-based survival with objective-based gameplay drawn from Cold War's Outbreak and Onslaught modes. While the Dark Aether narrative continued well, the stripped-back launch content and departure from traditional round-based maps drew criticism from long-time Zombies fans.


Warzone Integration

Raven Software led Vanguard's integration into Warzone, introducing the Pacific-themed Caldera map to replace Verdansk in December 2021. Vanguard's weapon pool and operators were added to Warzone alongside the map, though the integration was widely seen as bloated — with Caldera drawing mixed reception and Warzone's performance suffering under the weight of content from three different premium titles simultaneously.

Engine

The engine used in Call of Duty: Vanguard is 'S4', which is a modified IW8 engine, intended for easy integration of assets into Warzone.


Despite Sledgehammer's history of building their own in-house engines — a custom engine for Advanced Warfare, and an upgraded version for WWII — Vanguard marked a departure from that tradition. For this title, Sledgehammer adopted Infinity Ward's IW 8.0 engine, the same technology that powered MW2019 and Warzone.

This was a deliberate cross-studio decision driven largely by Warzone's ecosystem: since all post-2019 premium content needed to integrate with Warzone's IW8 base, building Vanguard on a separate engine would have created the same cross-engine headaches that plagued the Cold War integration. Sledgehammer's engineers expanded upon IW 8.0 with improvements to photogrammetry-based texture work, a new structural damage and destruction layer system, and enhanced volumetric lighting — making Vanguard visually the most technically polished IW8 title.

The move also set the stage for IW9 the following year: starting with Modern Warfare II (2022), Activision mandated a fully unified engine across all three lead studios, ending the era of each team shipping on its own codebase.

Development

Vanguard's path to release was shaped by Sledgehammer's own internal upheaval, a forced two-year support stint on other studios' titles, and the mounting pressure of Warzone's growing ecosystem.


Studio Rebuilding

Sledgehammer's previous lead title, WWII, had shipped in 2017 to strong commercial results — crossing $500 million in its first three days and $1 billion the following month. But the aftermath was turbulent. In February 2018, studio co-founders Glen Schofield and Michael Condrey both departed Sledgehammer, leaving the studio without its founding creative leadership for the first time in its history.

The years that followed were spent on support roles: Sledgehammer assisted Infinity Ward on MW2019 in 2019, and Treyarch on Black Ops Cold War in 2020 — the latter directly resulting from the studio conflict that had originally assigned Raven and Sledgehammer as co-leads before Activision intervened. Under new Studio Head Aaron Halon, Sledgehammer used this period to restructure internally, growing from around 200 staff in 2020 to over 450 by August 2021, with new offices opened in Melbourne, Toronto, and Guildford.


Return to Lead & Warzone Pressure

Confirmed as lead developer in May 2021 via Activision's Q1 earnings call, Sledgehammer had less than 18 months of public-facing development time before Vanguard shipped. The reveal came through Warzone's in-game Battle of Verdansk event on August 19, 2021, where the Vanguard trailer played at the event's conclusion.

The Warzone integration added significant scope to the project. Raven Software led the Warzone side of development, delivering the entirely new Caldera map — a Pacific island designed from scratch — alongside Vanguard's launch. The simultaneous delivery of a full premium title and a new Warzone map was an enormous undertaking, and Caldera's mixed reception at launch reflected the strain of that compressed timeline.


Reception & Legacy

Vanguard shipped to mixed-to-positive critical reviews and failed to meet Activision's internal sales expectations — a rare public admission from the publisher. Much of the criticism centred on the Zombies mode's shallow launch offering and the sense that, as the third consecutive WWII-themed Call of Duty, the setting had worn out its welcome. Caldera was replaced by the returning Rebirth Island and Al Mazrah over Warzone's subsequent seasons, with Vanguard's content cycle winding down ahead of Modern Warfare II's launch in late 2022.
